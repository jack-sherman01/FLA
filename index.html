FLA:
a force-language-action model for robot safe contact-rich manipulation

Paper Code Models

We introduce FLA, a open-source force-language-action model (FLA), fine-tuned on xx robot episodes from the SOTA OpenVLA with force input and safety constraints. FLA sets a new state of the art for generalist robot manipulation policies especially for contact-rich tasks. The FLA checkpoints and PyTorch training pipeline are fully open-source and models can be downloaded and fine-tuned from HuggingFace.

The FLA Model

We train FLA by fine-tuning the OpenVLA spatial. Our model consists of three key elements: (1) a fused force input, consisting of , (2) a set of contact-rich tasks that (3) a RL-fine-tuned backbone that predicts tokenized output actions. These tokens get decoded into continuous output actions that can be directly executed on the robot for contact-rich tasks.
